Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

This class taught me the importance of understanding how rhetoric shapes communication, how technology influences creative tools and processes, and whether ethics is responsible and influential in what we have created. I want to learn how to make a design that is effective, meaningful, and socially conscious by critically evaluating these factors.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Strong foundations of design principles such as balance, alignment, contrast, and hierarchical structure are essential for creating engaging and functional pieces. I applied these principles to the project, which helped me communicate clearly and engage the audience effectively.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Collaboration and feedback are essential to the creative process. Through a workshop-based environment, I can participate in peer criticism, group discussions, and collaboration exercises to gain different perspectives and improve my ability to give and receive constructive feedback.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Great design comes from repetition. Looking at other students' works, I feel my shortcomings through various media formats such as text, visual materials, multimedia, etc. Thus, I want to make a better design while fixing my shortcomings.

5. Learn to present your work.

Presenting a work is just as important as making a work. Through this process, I want the skills to clearly and confidently express my design choices, methods, and results. Through presentation and criticism, I want to develop my ability to present my work in a way that communicates and persuades the audience.
